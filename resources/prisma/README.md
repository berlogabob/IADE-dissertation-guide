# PRISMA Guidelines

## What is PRISMA?

PRISMA (Preferred Reporting Items for Systematic Reviews and Meta-Analyses) is an evidence-based set of items for reporting in systematic reviews and meta-analyses.

## PRISMA 2020 Checklist

The updated checklist includes 27 items organized by section:

### Title & Abstract
1. Title — Identify as systematic review
2. Abstract — Structured summary

### Introduction
3. Rationale — Describe rationale in context of existing knowledge
4. Objectives — Explicit statement of research questions

### Methods
5. Protocol — Registration information
6. Eligibility criteria — Inclusion/exclusion criteria
7. Information sources — Databases and sources searched
8. Search strategy — Full electronic search strategy
9. Selection process — How studies were selected
10. Data collection process — How data was extracted
11. Study variables — Outcomes and modifiers
12. Effect measures — Effect measures used
13. Synthesis methods — Data synthesis methods
14. Reporting bias assessment — Publication bias assessment
15. Certainty assessment — Certainty of evidence assessment

### Results
16. Study selection — PRISMA flow diagram
17. Study characteristics — Table of included studies
18. Risk of bias — Risk of bias results
19. Results of individual studies — Forest plot or equivalent
20. Results of syntheses — Summary of findings
21. Reporting biases — Publication bias assessment
22. Certainty of evidence — Summary of findings table

### Discussion
23. Discussion — Interpretation of results
24. Limitations — Limitations of the review
25. Implications — Implications for practice/policy/research

### Other
26. Funding — Sources of funding
27. Competing interests — Conflicts of interest
