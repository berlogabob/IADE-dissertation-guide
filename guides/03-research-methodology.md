# 03 — Research Methodology

## Overview

Your methodology chapter explains **how** you conducted your research. It must be rigorous enough that another researcher could replicate your study.

## Types of Research

### Quantitative

- Uses **numerical data** and statistical analysis
- Tests hypotheses using experiments, surveys, or existing datasets
- Common methods: experiments, surveys, statistical analysis

### Qualitative

- Uses **non-numerical data** (interviews, observations, documents)
- Explores meanings, experiences, and perspectives
- Common methods: case studies, interviews, ethnography, grounded theory

### Mixed Methods

- Combines quantitative and qualitative approaches
- Provides a more comprehensive understanding
- Requires justification for combining methods

## Steps to Define Your Methodology

### 1. Choose Your Approach

Based on your research question:
- **Exploratory** → Qualitative
- **Descriptive** → Either (depends on data)
- **Explanatory** → Quantitative
- **Comprehensive** → Mixed Methods

### 2. Define Your Data Collection

| Method | When to Use |
|--------|-------------|
| Survey/Questionnaire | Large sample, standardized data |
| Interviews | In-depth understanding, small sample |
| Experiments | Testing cause-effect relationships |
| Case Study | In-depth analysis of specific instance |
| Secondary Data | Using existing datasets |

### 3. Define Your Sample

- **Population** — Who/what you're studying
- **Sampling method** — How you select participants
- **Sample size** — How many (justify your choice)

### 4. Define Your Analysis

- **Quantitative**: Statistical tests (t-test, ANOVA, regression, etc.)
- **Qualitative**: Thematic analysis, coding, content analysis

### 5. Address Validity and Reliability

- **Internal validity** — Results are due to your intervention, not confounds
- **External validity** — Results generalize to other contexts
- **Reliability** — Results are consistent and reproducible

### 6. Ethical Considerations

- Informed consent
- Data protection and privacy
- Anonymity and confidentiality
- Institutional approval (if required)

## Writing Tips

- Write in **past tense** (you already did it)
- Be specific about every detail (tools, settings, dates)
- Justify every methodological choice
- Acknowledge limitations honestly

## Common Mistakes

| Mistake | Solution |
|---------|----------|
| Methods don't match research question | Re-align approach with your question |
| No justification for sample size | Use power analysis or cite similar studies |
| Vague description of analysis | Be specific about every step |
| Ignoring limitations | Address them directly in this chapter |
