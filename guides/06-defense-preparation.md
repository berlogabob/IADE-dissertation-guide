# 06 — Defense Preparation

## Overview

The defense (or viva) is your opportunity to present and justify your research to a panel of examiners. Good preparation is key.

## Before the Defense

### 1. Know Your Work Inside Out

- Be ready to explain **every decision** you made
- Understand the **limitations** of your study
- Know how your work **contributes** to the field
- Be familiar with **all references** you cited

### 2. Prepare Your Presentation

Typical structure (15–20 minutes):

1. **Introduction** (2 min) — Problem and motivation
2. **Objectives** (1 min) — What you set out to do
3. **Literature Review** (3 min) — Key findings and gap
4. **Methodology** (4 min) — How you did it
5. **Results** (5 min) — What you found
6. **Discussion & Conclusions** (3 min) — What it means
7. **Future Work** (1 min) — Next steps

### 3. Create Slides

- Use the LaTeX template: [`templates/dissertation/`](../templates/dissertation/)
- Keep slides **visual** (charts, diagrams, not walls of text)
- Include **key results** but save details for Q&A
- Practice with a timer

## Common Questions

Prepare answers for these:

### About Your Research
- Why did you choose this topic?
- What is your main contribution?
- What are the limitations of your study?
- How does your work relate to [specific paper]?
- What would you do differently?

### About Your Methodology
- Why did you choose this method?
- How did you select your sample?
- How did you ensure validity/reliability?
- What alternative methods did you consider?

### About Your Results
- Are your results statistically significant?
- How do your results compare with existing literature?
- What surprised you in the results?
- What are the practical implications?

### About Future Work
- What are the next steps?
- How would you extend this research?
- What funding or resources would you need?

## During the Defense

### Do
- Stay calm and confident
- Listen carefully to each question
- Answer honestly — it's okay to say "I don't know"
- Take a moment to think before answering
- Thank the panel for their questions

### Don't
- Get defensive about criticism
- Argue with examiners
- Read from your slides
- Rush through answers
- Make up answers you're not sure about

## After the Defense

- Address any required revisions promptly
- Submit final version by the deadline
- Archive your work for future reference

## Common Mistakes

| Mistake | Solution |
|---------|----------|
| Not knowing your own limitations | Acknowledge them proactively |
| Reading from slides | Practice until you can speak naturally |
| Going over time | Practice with a timer multiple times |
| Getting defensive | View questions as opportunities to clarify |
| Not preparing for methodology questions | Review every methodological choice |

## Good Luck!

You've done the hard work. Trust your preparation and present with confidence.
