# Connected Papers Guide

## What is Connected Papers?

Connected Papers creates a visual graph of related academic papers. It helps you discover relevant work by showing papers that are cited together (co-citation) or share references.

## How to Use

1. Go to [connectedpapers.com](https://www.connectedpapers.com/)
2. Search for a paper by title, DOI, or URL
3. Select your paper from the results
4. Explore the visual graph

## Understanding the Graph

- **Node size** = Number of citations
- **Node color** = Publication year (darker = newer)
- **Edges** = Papers that share references
- **Distance** = Relatedness (closer = more related)

## Two Views

### Prior Papers (Left)
Papers published **before** your seed paper that influenced it.

### Derivative Papers (Right)
Papers published **after** your seed paper that cite it or its references.

## Tips for Literature Review

1. Start with a **seminal paper** in your field
2. Explore both prior and derivative papers
3. Export relevant papers to Zotero/Mendeley
4. Use the graph to find **research gaps** (isolated clusters)

## Limitations

- Free plan: 5 graphs per month
- Covers most major databases but not all
- Best for Computer Science and Engineering

## Pricing

- **Free**: 5 graphs/month
- **Premium**: Unlimited graphs, collaboration features

## Alternatives

- [Research Rabbit](https://app.researchrabbit.ai/) — Free, unlimited
- [LitMaps](https://app.litmaps.com/) — Free/Premium
- [Inciteful](https://incitefulmed.com/academic/) — Free
