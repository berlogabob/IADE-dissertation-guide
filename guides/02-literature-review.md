# 02 - Literature Review

## Overview

A literature review synthesizes existing research on your topic. It demonstrates your understanding of the field and identifies the gap your research will fill.

## Steps

### 1. Define Your Search Strategy

- List **keywords** and synonyms for your research question
- Define **inclusion/exclusion criteria** (date range, language, study type)
- Choose which **databases** to search

### 2. Search the Databases

Use multiple sources for comprehensive coverage:

| Database | Best For |
|----------|----------|
| [Semantic Scholar](https://www.semanticscholar.org/) | AI-powered search, citations |
| [ACM Digital Library](https://dl.acm.org) | Computer Science |
| [IEEE Xplore](https://ieeexplore.ieee.org/Xplore/home.jsp) | Engineering, CS |
| [Springer](https://link.springer.com/) | Multi-disciplinary |
| [ScienceDirect](https://www.sciencedirect.com/browse/journals-and-books?subject=computer-science) | CS, Engineering |
| [Google Scholar](https://scholar.google.com/) | Broad coverage |
| [dblp](https://dblp.uni-trier.de/) | CS bibliography |

See the full list: [Digital Libraries](../resources/digital-libraries.md)

### 3. Manage Your References

Use a bibliography manager to organize papers:

- [Zotero](https://www.zotero.org/) → Recommended, free, desktop + web
- [Mendeley](https://www.mendeley.com/) → Alternative option
- [JabRef](https://www.jabref.org/) → Offline, open source

See: [Bibliography Managers](../resources/bibliography-managers.md)

### 4. Read and Take Notes

For each paper, record:
- **Research question/hypothesis**
- **Methodology** used
- **Key findings**
- **Limitations**
- **How it relates to your work**

### 5. Synthesize, Don't Just Summarize

Organize your review by **themes**, not by paper. Show how studies relate to each other and to your research question.

## Using AI Tools for Literature Review

These tools can accelerate your search:

- [Consensus](https://consensus.app/) → Find consensus across papers
- [Elicit](https://elicit.com/) → Extract findings from papers
- [Connected Papers](https://www.connectedpapers.com/) → Visualize paper relationships
- [Research Rabbit](https://app.researchrabbit.ai/) → Discover related papers

See: [AI Research Tools](../resources/ai-tools.md)

## Systematic Literature Review (SLR)

A formal SLR is **required** for your dissertation. Follow the methodology in:
- [SLR Guide](../resources/systematic-literature-review.md)
- [PRISMA Checklist](../resources/prisma/README.md)

## Common Mistakes

| Mistake | Solution |
|---------|----------|
| Only using Google Scholar | Search multiple databases |
| Reading everything | Focus on papers directly relevant to your question |
| Summarizing paper by paper | Synthesize by themes |
| Ignoring contradictory findings | Address conflicting results in your review |
