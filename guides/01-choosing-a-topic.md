# 01 - Choosing a Topic

## Overview

Choosing a dissertation topic is one of the most important decisions in your master's or PhD journey. A well-defined topic sets the foundation for your entire research.

## Steps

### 1. Identify Your Interests

- What subjects genuinely interest you?
- Which courses did you enjoy the most?
- What problems in your field do you want to solve?

### 2. Review Current Literature

Browse recent publications to identify:
- **Research gaps** → What hasn't been explored yet?
- **Emerging trends** → What topics are gaining traction?
- **Unresolved debates** → Where do experts disagree?

Use [digital libraries](../resources/digital-libraries.md) and [AI tools](../resources/ai-tools.md) to explore.

### 3. Assess Feasibility

Ask yourself:
- Can this be completed within the time limit?
- Do I have access to the necessary data/resources?
- Is the scope appropriate for a master's dissertation?

### 4. Formulate a Research Question

A good research question is:
- **Clear** → Specific and unambiguous
- **Focused** → Narrow enough to be manageable
- **Complex** → Requires analysis, not just description
- **Relevant** → Meaningful to your field

### 5. Write a Preliminary Title

Your title should:
- Reflect the scope of your study
- Include key variables or concepts
- Be concise (10–15 words maximum)

## Common Mistakes

| Mistake | Solution |
|---------|----------|
| Topic too broad | Narrow down to a specific aspect |
| Topic too narrow | Ensure enough literature exists |
| No clear research question | Refine until you can state it in one sentence |
| Choosing based on trend alone | Balance interest with feasibility |
